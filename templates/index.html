Boole's Rule Integration

Complete Step-by-Step Numerical Solver

Mathematical Discussion

1. Theoretical Foundation

Boole’s Rule is a member of the Newton-Cotes family of numerical integration. While the Trapezoidal rule uses straight lines and Simpson’s rule uses parabolas, Boole’s Rule uses a fourth-degree polynomial to approximate the integrand.

2. Why use Boole's Rule?

Boole's rule is chosen when high precision is required. It is exact for any polynomial of degree 5 or less. The error term is proportional to \(h^7\), meaning if you halve the step size, the error decreases by a factor of \(2^7 = 128\).

3. Error Analysis

Boole's error formula provides the theoretical bound or exact residual error when approximating a definite integral using Boole's Rule. It is typically expressed as:

\[ E = -\frac{8}{945} f^{(6)}(\xi)h^7 \]

Where:

  • \( h \) is the interval width evaluated as \( \frac{b-a}{n} \).
  • \( n \) is the number of sub-intervals (which must be a multiple of 4).
  • \( f^{(6)}(\xi) \) is the sixth derivative of the function being integrated, evaluated at some unknown point \( \xi \) in the interval \( (a, b) \).

5. Constraints

To apply Boole's Rule, the following must be true:

  • The function must be continuous on the interval [a, b].
  • The interval must be divided into exactly 4 equal sub-intervals (requiring 5 total points).
